Privacy Risks You Should Know

Understanding how your personal information is collected and used helps you make informed decisions about your digital life.

üì±

Location Tracking

Your movements are continuously tracked and stored, creating detailed profiles of your daily activities, work, and personal relationships.

Did you know? Google stores location data even when "Location History" is turned off.
üìß

Email Scanning

Your emails are automatically scanned for content, contacts, and behavioral patterns to build advertising profiles and improve AI systems.

Fact: Gmail scans every email for ad targeting, even after claiming to stop in 2017.
üîç

Search Profiling

Every search query builds a detailed profile of your interests, health concerns, political views, and personal relationships.

Reality: Google stores 20+ years of search history linked to your identity.
üéØ

Behavioral Prediction

AI systems predict your future behavior, purchasing decisions, and life events to influence your choices through targeted content.

Impact: Your data influences what information you see and decisions you make.
üë•

Social Graph Mapping

Detailed maps of your relationships, contacts, and social connections are created and analyzed for various purposes.

Scope: Google knows more about your relationships than you might realize.
üí∞

Data Monetization

Your personal information is the primary product being sold to advertisers, creating billions in revenue from your private life.

Truth: If the service is free, your data is the product being sold.
